GENERATIONS OF COMPUTER

The word'computer' comes from the word compute means 'to calculate' computers were developed from calculators to a more complex calculating machine, invented by different scientist who are called fathers of computer.

FIRST GENERATION(1940-1956) VACUUM TUBES

The first computers used vacuum tubes for circuitry and magnetic drums for memory. First generation computers relied on machine language the lowest level programming language understood by computers to perform operatios and they could only solve one problem at a time.

The inventor during first Generation

  • John Mauchly
  • Presper Eckert
  • John Von Neumann

SECOND GENERATION (1956-1963) TRANSISTOR

A transistor is a semiconductor device used to amplify and switch electronic signals and electriy power. It is composed of semiconductor material with at least three terminals for connection to an external circuit

The inventor during Second Generation

  • William Shockley
  • Walter Brattain

THIRD GENERATION (1964-1971) INTEGRATED CIRCUITS

An integrated circuit or monolithic integrated circuit (also referred to as ic,chip,or microchip) is an electronic circuit manufactured by the patterned diffusion of trace elements into the surface of a thin substrate of semiconductor material

The inventor during Third Generation

  • Jack Kilby
  • Robert Noyce

FOURTH GENERATION (1971-PRESENT) MICROPROCESSOR

The microprocessor brought the fourth generation of computers that could fit on the palm of the hand. Microprocessor also moved out of the realm of desktop computers

The inventor during Fourth Generation

  • Ted Hoff

FIFTH GENERATION (PRESENT AND BEYOND) ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

Fifth generation computers are in developed stage which is based on the artificial intelligence

The inventor during Fifth Generation

  • John McCarthy

SIXTH GENERATION ROBOTICS

It is an enhancement of the fifth generation, the AI Robotics is a concept in programming which enables the device to think and take actions on their own. The sixth generation of computers is said to have a good resemblance of the human brain
